# Map Resolution & Nation Count Guide

This guide helps you choose the right map resolution based on the number of players you want to support.

**Important:** Resolution refers to the **longest side** of your map. For example, a 2048px resolution means the longest dimension (width or height) will be 2048 pixels, and the shorter side will be scaled proportionally.

## Resolution Options


### 512px
- **Recommended nation count:** 2-8 nations
- **Best for:** Very small maps, quick games, 1v1 or tiny team battles
- **Description:** Compact maps perfect for fast-paced gameplay (longest side = 512px)
- **Note:** No official maps use this resolution - consider using 1024px or 1536px instead



### 1024px
- **Recommended nation count:** 12-29 nations
- **Best for:** Medium-sized maps, regional conflicts
- **Description:** Suitable for detailed regional maps (longest side = 1024px)
- **Examples:** Bosphorus Straits (1000x612, 12 nations), Caucasus (1250x1000, 21 nations), Pangaea (1000x1000, 29 nations)

### 1536px
- **Recommended nation count:** 2-26 nations (average: 10 nations)
- **Best for:** Medium to large maps with varied gameplay
- **Description:** Most flexible resolution - works for everything from small tournament maps to regional conflicts (longest side = 1536px)
- **Examples:** 
  - Small: Bering Strait (1500x918, 2 nations), Tourney maps (1500x1500, 2-8 nations)
  - Medium: Black Sea (1500x1100, 9 nations), Didier (1500x1350, 12 nations), Montreal (1530x1500, 12 nations)
  - Large: East Asia (1562x1646, 22 nations), Gulf of St Lawrence (1620x1350, 26 nations)

### 2048px
- **Recommended nation count:** 4-61 nations (average: 20 nations)
- **Best for:** Large maps, continental conflicts, long games
- **Description:** Most popular resolution for serious multiplayer - high detail maps for extended gameplay (longest side = 2048px)
- **Examples:** 
  - Small: Achiran (2000x1700, 4 nations), Two Lakes (2100x1840, 5 nations), Mars (2000x1000, 6 nations)
  - Medium: Alps (2000x1837, 8 nations), Iceland (2000x1500, 8 nations), The Box (2048x2048, 13 nations)
  - Large: Europe Classic (2000x1000, 31 nations), Oceania (2000x1000, 32 nations), Great Lakes (2000x1300, 34 nations), Africa (1950x2032, 36 nations)
  - Very Large: Didier France (2100x2250, 42 nations), World (2000x1000, 61 nations)

### 2560px
- **Recommended nation count:** 11-49 nations (average: 23 nations)
- **Best for:** Very large maps, major continental regions
- **Description:** Detailed continental-scale maps for serious strategic gameplay (longest side = 2560px)
- **Examples:** Baikal (2500x1565, 11 nations), Japan (2500x2500, 12 nations), Conakry (2457x1000, 20 nations), South America (1746x2378, 24 nations), North America (2800x1448, 49 nations)

### 3072px
- **Recommended nation count:** 7-66 nations (average: 33 nations)
- **Best for:** Huge maps, multi-continental conflicts
- **Description:** Maximum detail for large-scale strategic gameplay (longest side = 3072px)
- **Examples:** Strait of Gibraltar (2902x1477, 7 nations), Hawaii (3200x2076, 9 nations), Luna (1310x3500, 25 nations), Mediterranean (2850x1450, 38 nations), Europe (2905x1674, 50 nations), Dyslexdria (3083x1344, 66 nations)
- **Note:** Includes some very long/narrow maps like Luna (1310x3500)

### 4096px
- **Recommended nation count:** 16-97+ nations (average: 45 nations)
- **Best for:** Massive maps, world-scale conflicts, special formats
- **Description:** Extremely large maps (longest side = 4096px+) - warning: slow processing and high memory usage
- **Examples:** Passage (6000x400, 16 nations), Amazon River (5537x279, 21 nations), Giant World Map (4110x1948, 97 nations)
- **Note:** Processing may be very slow, and the map will require significant browser resources. Some maps in this range are extremely long/narrow.

## Tips

- Start with **Auto** or **1536px** for your first maps
- Use **1024px** for quick regional maps (12-29 nations)
- Choose **2048px** for most serious multiplayer games (the most popular choice)
- Use **2560px** for continental-scale maps
- Only use **3072px** or **4096px** if you need a truly massive world map
- Consider your target audience's hardware capabilities
- Remember: resolution = longest side of your image
